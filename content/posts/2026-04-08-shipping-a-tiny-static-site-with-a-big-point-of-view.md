Small sites do not need to look temporary. A static stack can still feel intentional when the structure is clear,
the visual language is consistent, and the content model is honest about what the site actually needs.

This portfolio uses plain HTML, CSS, and JavaScript because the job is straightforward: introduce a person,
provide a resume snapshot, publish writing, and make contact easy. Extra tooling would add ceremony without improving the outcome.

## What the smaller stack buys you

It keeps deployment simple, maintenance approachable, and future edits lightweight. A new post is just a new file.
A visual adjustment is one stylesheet edit. The result is a site that stays easy to reason about even as it grows.

The constraint is manual upkeep, but for a small personal site that tradeoff can be a strength. Simplicity keeps the publishing loop short.
