Loud interfaces get attention quickly, but they often become tiring just as fast. Calm interfaces do a quieter job:
they help the important things stay visible, preserve rhythm, and leave enough room for the content to lead.

That does not mean plain or lifeless. It means choosing a point of view without forcing every surface to shout.
Contrast, spacing, hierarchy, and motion still matter, but they work best when they support comprehension instead of competing for it.

## What tends to hold up

In practice, the interfaces that age well usually share the same traits: strong information structure, readable text,
deliberate spacing, and a small set of visual rules repeated consistently. The result is memorable without being exhausting.
